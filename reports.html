Harvard Medical School Fundamentals

Overview: This course provides a deep dive into the physiological principles underlying human health and disease. It bridges the gap between basic science and clinical application.

Key Modules Covered:

  • Immunology: Mechanisms of innate and adaptive immunity, immune deficiencies, and hypersensitivity reactions.
  • Physiology: Cardiovascular dynamics, respiratory mechanics, and renal filtration processes.
  • Genetics: Mendelian inheritance, chromosomal abnormalities, and modern genetic testing methods.

Clinical Application:

The course emphasizes how understanding molecular mechanisms leads to better diagnostic reasoning. For example, understanding the cytokine storm in sepsis helps in managing shock patients.
